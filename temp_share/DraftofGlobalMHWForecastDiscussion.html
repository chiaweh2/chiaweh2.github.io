Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - March 2024

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:        

In February 2024, 38% [24%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 5th [5th] among all months since 1991.

Widespread marine heatwaves (MHWs) are currently found in the central equatorial Pacific, along the U.S. west coast, in portions of the northwest Pacific, Sea of Japan, and South China Sea, much of the tropical and eastern Atlantic, the Caribbean, south of Iceland, the Mediterranean Sea, much of the tropical Indian Ocean, and portions of the Atlantic, Indian and Pacific sectors of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage will decline in the coming months (especially in the eastern tropical Pacific, Atlantic and Indian basins), dropping to ~32% [15%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by June-August 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions, associated with a dissipating El Niño event, are forecasted to decrease in the eastern tropical Pacific but persist through April 2024 in the central Pacific (70-100% [30-90%]; high confidence), with the likelihood of MHWs and intensity of temperature anomalies decreasing through the end of the year. In the western Indo-Pacific region, elevated MHW likelihoods are forecasted to persist through northern fall 2024 (50-80% [30-70%]; medium confidence).
  • North Pacific - Forecasts show limited risk of MHW conditions along the west coast of North America through summer (10-40% [0-30%]; high confidence). In the central North Pacific, MHWs are forecasted to persist through September (50-80% [20-50%]; high confidence), as are MHWs near Japan (60-90% [20-50%]; low confidence).
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are likely to persist through at least September 2024 (70-100% [50-80%]; medium-to-high confidence). MHWs in the tropical South Atlantic are expected to diminish through boreal spring.
  • Caribbean Sea - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist in the Caribbean Sea through September (70-100% [50-80%]; medium-to-high confidence).
  • North Atlantic - High MHW probabilities remain through April in the northeast Atlantic from Northern Africa to Europe and extending northwestward to Greenland (60-100% [30-70%]; low confidence). An elevated risk of MHWs continues in the eastern part of the basin through December 2024, although at a lower probability (40-90% [30-70%]; low confidence). There is also an enhanced likelihood of MHWs off the US east coast in the later half of the year (30-60% [10-30%]; low confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted in the western Indian Ocean through June 2024 (60-100% [40-70%]; medium-to-high confidence), with the highest probabilities off the east African coast north of Madagascar and  in the southern Arabian Sea.
  • Southern Ocean - MHWs in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to be most likely south of Africa (70-100% [30-80%]; low confidence) and to a lesser degree south of Australia for April 2024 (40-90% [10-50%]; low confidence).
