Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - December 2023

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

In November 2023, 39% [25%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 1st [5th] among all months since 1991.

Widespread marine heatwaves (MHW) are currently found in the equatorial Pacific, west of Baja California, in a band extending from the US west coast southwestward towards Hawaii and beyond, in portions of the northwest Pacific, the Sea of Japan, the South China Sea, the Caribbean Sea, much of the tropical Atlantic, in the vicinity of Greenland, in a narrow band in the northwest Atlantic (likely associated with the Gulf Stream/North Atlantic Current) the western Indian Ocean, and several sectors (Indian, Pacific, Atlantic) of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage will remain elevated with more than 35% [20%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions through January of 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Eastern Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions, associated with a strong El Niño event, are forecasted to persist through January of 2024 (80-100% [50-100%]; high confidence), with the intensity of the anomalies exceeding 1.5℃. MHW chances are expected to decline in this region by March of 2024, especially near the equator.
  • North Pacific - Forecasts show an elevated risk of MHW conditions developing in some nearshore regions along the west coast of North America over the next 2-3 months (30-60% [20-40%]; medium confidence). In the central North Pacific, MHWs are forecasted to persist through northern winter (50-80% [20-50%]; medium confidence), as are MHWs near Japan (50-80% [20-50%]; low confidence). MHWs in the South China Sea are projected to continue for several seasons (50-80% [30-60%] chance through September; medium confidence)
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical Atlantic are forecasted to persist through at least June 2024, especially to the north of the equator (70-100% [50-80%]; medium-to-high confidence)
  • North Atlantic - High probabilities remain in the waters south of Iceland through March 2024 (60-100% [40-80%]; medium confidence).
  • Caribbean Sea - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist through at least June 2024 (60-90% [50-70%]; medium-to-high confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - MHW conditions are likely to persist in the western Indian Ocean through at least March 2024 (70-100% [50-100%]; medium-to-high confidence), with the highest probabilities off the east African coast north of Madagascar and  in the southern Arabian Sea.
  • Southern Ocean - MHWs in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to be most likely in the Atlantic and Indian Ocean sectors south of Africa with probabilities of extending to at least March 2024 (50-80% [40-70%] low-medium confidence) .
