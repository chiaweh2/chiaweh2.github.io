Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - August 2023

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

Approximately 48% [30%] of the global ocean is currently experiencing MHWs, which ranks 1st [3rd] among all months since 1991.

Widespread marine heatwaves (MHW) are currently found in the equatorial Pacific, the northeast Pacific, the northwest Pacific and the Sea of Japan, Sea of Okhotsk, the tropical North Atlantic, the Caribbean Sea, the Gulf of Mexico, the northwest Atlantic/southern Labrador Sea, Northeast Atlantic from northern Africa to Spain, along the southern rim of Arctic Ocean, the southwest Pacific near New Zealand, the Pacific west of South America, portions of the Southern Indian Ocean, and all sectors (Indian, Pacific, Atlantic) of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage will remain near 50% [decrease slightly to 25%] of the global oceans through February of 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Eastern Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist through the end of the year (70-90% [50-80%] chance; high confidence), with the intensity of the anomalies also forecasted to grow as El Niño develops and reaches a maximum. MHW conditions extend into the central/western tropical Pacific (60-90% [30-60% ] chance; medium confidence)
  • North Pacific - The MHW off the Oregon and Washington coasts are forecasted to weaken over the next 1-2 months. However, forecasts also show elevated risk of MHWs along the U.S. west coast and Gulf of Alaska in northern hemisphere spring 2024 (40-70% [20-60%] chance; medium confidence).  In the central North Pacific, MHWs offshore are forecasted to persist through the end of the year (50-80% [40-60%] chance; low confidence) and the Northwest Pacific near Japan (50-80% [20-50%] chance; low confidence).
  • Arctic - MHWs persist across much of the southern Arctic through November (70-90% [20-50%]; high confidence) primarily due to large-scale warming and a decrease in sea ice.
  • Southwest Pacific - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist off the southeast coast of New Zealand through October 2023 (60-90% [20-40%] chance; medium confidence) with MHW likelihood falling by the end of the year.
  • North Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are forecasted to persist through September 2023 (70-100% [40-70%] chance; low-medium confidence), and possibly through the end of 2023 (50-80% [20-50%] chance; low confidence). Around Iceland, MHW conditions are forecasted to persist through September 2023 (40-60% [30-50%] chance; low confidence). MHWs near Newfoundland are expected to diminish over the next 1-2 months (low-to-medium confidence).
  • Gulf of Mexico/Caribbean Sea - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist in the  Caribbean Sea and southern Gulf of Mexico, including around southern Florida, through October 2023 (70-100% [40-80%] chance; medium confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - MHW conditions are forecasted to persist in the western Indian Ocean through September 2023 (70-90% [30-60% chance; medium confidence), with high MHW likelihood between the Southern India and the Horn of Africa continuing through the end of the year (50-90% [30-70%] chance; medium confidence).
