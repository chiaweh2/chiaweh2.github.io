Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - January 2023

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

In December 2023, 37% [24%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 4th [6th] among all months since 1991.

Widespread marine heatwaves (MHW) are currently found in the eastern equatorial Pacific, along the U.S. west coast, in portions of the northwest Pacific, Sea of Japan, and South China Sea, much of the tropical, northeast, and southeast Atlantic, south of Iceland, the Mediterranean Sea, the western Indian Ocean, and several sectors (Indian, Pacific, Atlantic) of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage will remain elevated but will decline, dropping to ~30% [15%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by May-June 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Eastern Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions, associated with a strong El Niño event, are forecasted to persist through March 2024 (70-100% [30-80%]; high confidence), with the likelihood of MHWs and intensity of anomalies decreasing through boreal spring. In the Indo-Pacific region, elevated MHW likelihoods are persistent through boreal summer 2024 (70-90% [40-70%]; medium confidence).
  • North Pacific - Forecasts show an elevated risk of MHW conditions along the west coast of North America through March (30-60% [20-50%]; medium confidence). In the central North Pacific, MHWs are forecasted to persist through March (50-90% [20-50%]; medium confidence), as are MHWs near Japan (50-80% [20-50%]; low confidence).
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical Atlantic are likely to persist through at least June 2024, especially to the north of the equator (70-90% [50-70%]; medium-to-high confidence)
  • North Atlantic - High MHW probabilities remain through April in the northeast Atlantic from Northern Africa to Europe and extending northwest to Greenland (40-90% [30-70%]; low confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted in the western Indian Ocean through April 2024 (80-100% [40-90%]; medium-to-high confidence), with the highest probabilities off the east African coast north of Madagascar and  in the southern Arabian Sea.
  • Southern Ocean - MHWs in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to be most likely in the south of Africa and Australia, with probabilities of 50-90% [30-60%] (low-medium confidence) .
