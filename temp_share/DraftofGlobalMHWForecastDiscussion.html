Global Marine Heatwave Forecast Discussion - February 2024

Observed and forecasted values include the effects of long-term warming since 1990. Values with the long-term warming trend removed are in brackets.

Current marine heatwave conditions:

In January 2024, 41% [26%] of the global ocean experienced MHWs, which ranked 1st [3rd] among all months since 1991.

Widespread marine heatwaves (MHW) are currently found in the eastern equatorial Pacific, along the U.S. west coast, in portions of the northwest Pacific, Sea of Japan, and South China Sea, much of the tropical, northeast, and southeast Atlantic, south of Iceland, the Mediterranean Sea, the western Indian Ocean, and the Indian and Pacific sectors of the Southern Ocean.

Marine heatwave forecasts:

Forecasts predict that MHW coverage will decline in the coming months (especially in the Atlantic and Indian basins), dropping to ~32% [17%] of the global oceans experiencing MHW conditions by May-June 2024. Below is a regionally refined focus:

  • Tropical Pacific - MHW conditions, associated with a strong El Niño event, are forecasted to persist through March 2024 (70-100% [30-90%]; high confidence), with the likelihood of MHWs and intensity of anomalies decreasing through boreal spring. In the western Indo-Pacific region, elevated MHW likelihoods are forecasted to persist through boreal summer 2024 (70-90% [30-70%]; medium confidence).
  • North Pacific - Forecasts show an elevated risk of MHW conditions along the west coast of North America through March (40-60% [10-40%]; high confidence). In the central North Pacific, MHWs are forecasted to persist through March (60-90% [20-50%]; high confidence), as are MHWs near Japan (60-90% [20-50%]; low confidence).
  • Tropical Atlantic - MHW conditions in the tropical North Atlantic are likely to persist through at least June 2024 (70-100% [60-90%]; medium-to-high confidence). MHWs in the tropical South Atlantic are expected to diminish through boreal spring.
  • Caribbean Sea - In the next 3-4 months, the center of action for MHW conditions is forecasted to shift westward from the tropical North Atlantic into the Caribbean Sea and to the north of South America (70-100% [60-80%]; medium-to-high confidence).
  • North Atlantic - High MHW probabilities remain through April in the northeast Atlantic from Northern Africa to Europe and extending northwest to Greenland (60-100% [30-70%]; low confidence).
  • Indian Ocean - Elevated chances of MHW conditions are predicted in the western Indian Ocean through May 2024 (70-100% [40-80%]; medium-to-high confidence), with the highest probabilities off the east African coast north of Madagascar and  in the southern Arabian Sea.
  • Southern Ocean - MHWs in the Southern Ocean are forecasted to be most likely in the south of Africa and Australia for April 2024, with probabilities of 70-100% [30-80%] (low confidence) .
